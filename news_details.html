DAR AL ARAKAN COMPANY ANNOUNCES NEGOTIATIONS WITH MINISTRY OF HOUSING

Dar Al-Arkan Real Estate Development Company (DAAR) is a Saudi joint-stock company, founded in 1994. The Company operates solely in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and is mainly engaged in the business of real estate development, in addition to property management, lease, and investment activities. From the head office in the capital city of Riyadh, DAAR runs its operations along with the regional offices in Mecca, Jeddah, and Medina.

Dar Al-Arkan has emerged as a promising enterprise, backed by high financial and deep-established intellectual capabilities, and supported by the accumulated experience and foresight of its founders. Within few years following the growing investments and success, DAAR turned into a limited liability company in 2000 and managed to take the lead in the real estate sector. At that point, Dar Al-Arkan adopted creative, strategic goals and futuristic visions aiming to promote a renewable approach of real estate development. The Company has established the foundation for making a quantum leap in the concept of integrated urban communities that meet the aspirations of the community members and their growing needs as well as matching the best-in-class standards. Such strategic goals required an unprecedented expansion in the DAAR’s business.

Dar Al-Arkan, then, experienced a new strategic shift into a joint stock company in 2005 with a capital of SR 5.4 billion, bringing the Company on top of the listed real estate companies in the Saudi Capital Market. This transformation accompanied several changes made by the Company to advance the administrative systems and develop human capital, which provided additional impetus and created a favorable environment to carry out more activities in the market, and given more trust and respect among customers.

Dar Al-Arkan strives to implement all investment activities based on the main pillars; a belief that the development projects are not only meant for their techno-economic feasibility, but they also represent a noble goal to improve the quality of the life of the society members and the environment where they live. Attaining higher growth rates necessitates the achievement of the customers and shareholders goals as well as the Company’s objectives, which ensure the public interest without giving preference to the benefit of any party over another.

Moreover, Dar Al-Arkan is pressing ahead to attain the economic, social, health, environmental and security objectives of our developed communities and real estate products with a vision of raising people’s standards of living quantitatively and qualitatively, expressing our culture and preserving our heritage which reflects the uniqueness of our Saudi Muslim society.

As the Kingdom’s leading real estate company, it is the responsibility of Dar Al-Arkan to do an efficient national economic input in the housing sector. DAAR contributes to the expansion and increase of real estate production which leads to organizing, developing, and growing the national real estate market. Further, the provision of society needs for established real estate products will maximize supply-demand equilibrium. Such goals require the integration and enhancement of the real estate market strength factors through partnering with the concerned government agencies to organize, protect, and enhance the real estate market drivers as well as removing obstacles to avoid restricting capability and support market growth.

Dar Al-Arkan adopts three strategic business plans; real estate development, property management, and investments related to real estate. The Company employs various strategies, depending on the project, market conditions, and feasibility of the investment which in turn depends on the social, economic, commercial, and financial factors. Driven by its vision of becoming the pioneer real estate solutions provider, DAAR pursues “comprehensive urban development approach” in developing its real estate projects. The Company sells a share of the residential estates but retains other housing and commercial properties in the portfolio of investment assets for rental purposes, as part of Company’s strategy of diversification to expand its assets’ base.
